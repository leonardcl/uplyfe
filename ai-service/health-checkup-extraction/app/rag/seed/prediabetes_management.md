# Prediabetes — what the numbers mean and what helps most

Source: ADA Standards of Care in Diabetes (2024); Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) trial — NEJM 2002.

Prediabetes is the range below diabetes where intervention is most effective. The Diabetes Prevention Program showed a roughly 58 percent reduction in progression to diabetes over 3 years with intensive lifestyle change — larger than the effect of metformin in the same trial.

## Threshold reminders

- Fasting glucose 100 to 125 mg/dL: impaired fasting glucose.
- HbA1c 5.7 to 6.4 percent: prediabetes.
- 2-hour 75 g OGTT 140 to 199 mg/dL: impaired glucose tolerance.

A single value doesn't diagnose anything — confirm with a clinician.

## Diet anchors

- Carb quality matters more than carb total. Choose whole grains, legumes, vegetables, and fruit over refined flour, sugary drinks, and sweets.
- Eat fiber with carbs. Combining starchy foods with vegetables, beans, or nuts blunts the post-meal glucose spike.
- Sweetened beverages are the single largest avoidable driver. Replacing sugary drinks with water has a measurable effect on fasting glucose within weeks.
- Pace your meals. Skipping breakfast then large dinners worsens overnight and morning glucose for many people.
- Mediterranean and DASH eating patterns lowered progression to diabetes in multiple trials (PREDIMED 2013, DASH 2005).

## Activity anchors

- 150 minutes per week of moderate aerobic activity is the DPP target.
- A short walk after meals (10 to 15 minutes) reliably blunts the post-meal glucose rise.
- Add resistance training on 2 or more days per week — muscle is the largest tissue that takes up glucose.

## Weight

In the DPP, a 7 percent weight loss was the goal and produced the largest reduction in progression to type 2 diabetes. Even 3 to 5 percent helps.

## Sleep and stress

Sleep less than 7 hours and chronic stress both raise fasting glucose. Address these alongside food and activity rather than after.

## When to recheck

Most clinicians recommend rechecking glucose and HbA1c every 6 to 12 months once a prediabetes finding is identified.
