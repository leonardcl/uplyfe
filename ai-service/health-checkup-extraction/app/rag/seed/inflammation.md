# Inflammation markers — interpretation notes

Source: AHA/CDC scientific statement on hs-CRP; AACC ESR reference.

## hs-CRP

High-sensitivity C-reactive protein bands for cardiovascular risk:

- <1.0 mg/L — lower-risk band
- 1.0–3.0 mg/L — average-risk band
- >3.0 mg/L — higher-risk band

Values >10 mg/L typically reflect acute infection or another non-cardiac inflammatory process and are not specific for cardiovascular risk in that range.

## ESR

Typical adult upper limits (mm/hr):

- Men <50 yrs: ~15
- Men ≥50 yrs: ~20
- Women <50 yrs: ~20
- Women ≥50 yrs: ~30

ESR is a non-specific marker. It can rise with infection, autoimmune disease, malignancy, anemia, pregnancy, and obesity. Marked elevation (>100 mm/hr) has a narrower differential and warrants clinical evaluation.

## Practical context

Both CRP and ESR can be temporarily elevated for days to weeks after infections, surgery, or trauma. A single elevated value should be interpreted alongside the clinical context.
