# Complete blood count — interpretation notes

Source: MedlinePlus; AACC adult reference intervals.

## Hemoglobin and hematocrit

Typical adult ranges:

- Men: hemoglobin 13.5–17.5 g/dL; hematocrit 38.8–50.0%
- Women: hemoglobin 12.0–15.5 g/dL; hematocrit 34.9–44.5%

Anemia is hemoglobin below the sex-specific lower limit. Severe anemia (hemoglobin <8 g/dL) typically warrants prompt evaluation, particularly with symptoms such as marked fatigue, shortness of breath, chest pain, or dizziness.

Causes of anemia include iron deficiency, chronic disease, blood loss, B12/folate deficiency, hemolysis, and bone-marrow disorders. The MCV (red-cell size) helps narrow the cause:

- MCV <80 fL — microcytic; classic causes are iron deficiency and thalassemia trait.
- MCV 80–100 — normocytic.
- MCV >100 — macrocytic; classic causes include B12 / folate deficiency, alcohol, and some medications.

## WBC

Typical adult range: 4.5–11.0 ×10³/µL. Mild elevations are common with infection, stress, exercise, smoking, and corticosteroid use. Marked elevation (>30) or marked depression (<2) warrants prompt evaluation.

## Platelets

Typical adult range: 150–450 ×10³/µL. Severe thrombocytopenia (<50) carries bleeding risk; severe thrombocytosis (>1000) carries thrombosis risk and warrants evaluation.

## Practical notes

Reference ranges and the meaning of borderline values vary by lab, age, and recent illness. Any new abnormality should be repeated and discussed with a clinician before drawing conclusions.
