# Lipid panel — interpretation notes

Source: NCEP Adult Treatment Panel III; AHA/ACC 2018 Cholesterol Guideline.

## Total cholesterol

- <200 mg/dL desirable
- 200–239 mg/dL borderline-high
- ≥240 mg/dL high

Total cholesterol alone is a weak predictor; LDL, HDL, triglycerides, and non-HDL together give a better picture of cardiovascular risk.

## LDL ("bad" cholesterol)

- <100 optimal; <70 often the goal in people with established cardiovascular disease.
- 100–129 near-optimal
- 130–159 borderline-high
- 160–189 high
- ≥190 very high — discuss management with a clinician.

## HDL ("good" cholesterol)

Lower HDL is associated with higher cardiovascular risk:

- Men <40, women <50 mg/dL — low
- ≥60 — protective

## Triglycerides

- <150 normal
- 150–199 borderline-high
- 200–499 high
- ≥500 very high — pancreatitis risk rises substantially when ≥1000.

## Non-HDL cholesterol

Non-HDL = total cholesterol − HDL. It captures all atherogenic particles, not just LDL. Targets typically run 30 mg/dL above the LDL goal.

## Patterns to recognize

A combined picture of high LDL, high triglycerides, and low HDL — sometimes called "mixed dyslipidemia" — carries higher risk than any single value alone, and is common alongside elevated fasting glucose, central adiposity, and elevated blood pressure (metabolic-syndrome pattern).
