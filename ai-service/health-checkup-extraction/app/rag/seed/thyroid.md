# Thyroid panel — interpretation notes

Source: American Thyroid Association adult and pregnancy guidelines.

## TSH (thyroid-stimulating hormone)

Typical adult range: 0.4–4.0 mIU/L (some labs use 0.5–5.0). TSH is the most sensitive screening test:

- TSH high with low free T4 → overt hypothyroidism pattern.
- TSH high with normal free T4 → subclinical hypothyroidism.
- TSH low with high free T4/T3 → overt hyperthyroidism pattern.
- TSH low with normal free T4/T3 → subclinical hyperthyroidism.

TSH ≥10 mIU/L with normal free T4 is more often associated with progression to overt hypothyroidism than a milder elevation.

## Free T4 / Free T3

Free T4 (typical 0.8–1.8 ng/dL) and free T3 (typical 2.3–4.2 pg/mL) reflect circulating active hormone. They are interpreted alongside TSH.

## Special situations

Pregnancy uses trimester-specific TSH ranges; non-pregnant cutoffs do not apply. Acute illness can transiently lower thyroid hormone levels ("sick euthyroid"). Biotin supplements can interfere with the assay — clinicians often ask people to stop biotin for 2–3 days before testing.

A single abnormal TSH in an otherwise well person is usually repeated before any clinical decision.
