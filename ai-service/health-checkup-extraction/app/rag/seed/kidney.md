# Kidney function — interpretation notes

Source: KDIGO 2024 CKD Guideline; CKD-EPI 2021 (Inker et al., NEJM 2021).

## Creatinine and eGFR

Serum creatinine reflects kidney filtration but is also influenced by muscle mass, age, sex, and diet. eGFR (estimated glomerular filtration rate) corrects for those when calculated with the CKD-EPI 2021 equation.

KDIGO eGFR categories (mL/min/1.73m²):

- ≥90 — G1, normal/high
- 60–89 — G2, mildly decreased
- 45–59 — G3a, moderately decreased
- 30–44 — G3b, moderately decreased
- 15–29 — G4, severely decreased
- <15 — G5, kidney failure range

A single low eGFR is not chronic kidney disease; CKD requires sustained reduction (≥3 months) and/or markers of damage such as albuminuria.

## BUN

Blood urea nitrogen rises with reduced kidney filtration, dehydration, high-protein intake, gastrointestinal bleeding, and corticosteroids. It falls with low protein intake and severe liver disease. Interpret alongside creatinine; the BUN/creatinine ratio can help locate the cause.

## Uric acid

Hyperuricemia (men >7 mg/dL, women >6 mg/dL) is associated with gout and kidney stones. Asymptomatic hyperuricemia does not always require treatment.

## Practical notes

A new low eGFR in someone with vomiting, diuretic use, or NSAID exposure is often reversible with hydration and medication review. Persistent values <60 warrant evaluation for chronic kidney disease.
