# Glucose and HbA1c — interpretation notes

Source: American Diabetes Association (ADA) Standards of Care in Diabetes (2024); MedlinePlus.

## Fasting plasma glucose

Fasting means no caloric intake for ≥8 hours. ADA classifies fasting plasma glucose:

- <100 mg/dL — normal
- 100–125 mg/dL — impaired fasting glucose (prediabetes)
- ≥126 mg/dL — diabetes range; a single reading is not a diagnosis. Confirmation is typically required on a separate day.

A fasting value <70 mg/dL is hypoglycemia; <54 mg/dL is severe hypoglycemia and warrants prompt evaluation if symptomatic.

## HbA1c

HbA1c reflects average glucose over the prior 2–3 months. It is not affected by recent meals.

- <5.7% — normal
- 5.7–6.4% — prediabetes
- ≥6.5% — diabetes range; confirm with a clinician.

HbA1c can be falsely low or high in conditions affecting red-cell turnover (anemia, hemoglobinopathies, recent transfusion, pregnancy). When HbA1c and fasting glucose disagree, the disagreement itself is informative.

## Postprandial / OGTT

A 2-hour postprandial or OGTT value:

- <140 mg/dL — normal
- 140–199 mg/dL — impaired glucose tolerance
- ≥200 mg/dL — diabetes range.

## Practical context

A single elevated value should be repeated before any conclusion. Stress, illness, recent steroid use, and inadequate fasting all raise glucose. Discuss any abnormal value with a clinician — interpretation depends on the whole panel and personal history.

A glucose ≥400 mg/dL or HbA1c ≥10% is markedly elevated and warrants prompt evaluation.
