# BMI, waist, and blood pressure — interpretation notes

Sources: CDC adult BMI; NCEP ATP III waist criteria; ACC/AHA 2017 hypertension guideline.

## BMI

Adult BMI categories (kg/m²):

- <18.5 underweight
- 18.5–24.9 normal
- 25.0–29.9 overweight
- 30.0–34.9 obesity class I
- 35.0–39.9 obesity class II
- ≥40 obesity class III

WHO recommends lower cutoffs for some Asian populations: overweight ≥23, obesity ≥27.5. BMI is a screening tool; it does not measure body composition and may misclassify very muscular individuals or older adults with sarcopenia.

## Waist circumference

Elevated central adiposity (NCEP ATP III): men ≥102 cm (40 in), women ≥88 cm (35 in). Waist correlates with metabolic risk independently of BMI.

## Blood pressure

ACC/AHA 2017 categories (mmHg):

- <120 / <80 — normal
- 120–129 / <80 — elevated
- 130–139 / 80–89 — stage 1 hypertension
- ≥140 / ≥90 — stage 2 hypertension
- ≥180 / ≥120 — hypertensive crisis

A blood-pressure diagnosis is based on multiple readings on separate days; a single elevated reading is a flag, not a diagnosis. Hypertensive crisis with chest pain, shortness of breath, vision changes, or neurologic symptoms is a medical emergency.

## Why this matters together

Central adiposity, elevated triglycerides, low HDL, elevated fasting glucose, and elevated blood pressure travel together as the metabolic-syndrome pattern. Three or more of these markers identify a population at higher cardiovascular and diabetes risk.
