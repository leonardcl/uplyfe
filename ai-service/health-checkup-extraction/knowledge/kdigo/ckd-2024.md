# KDIGO 2024 — Evaluation and management of CKD

Source: Kidney Disease: Improving Global Outcomes. 2024 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Evaluation and Management of CKD. https://kdigo.org/guidelines/ckd-evaluation-and-management/
This is a paraphrased summary for retrieval, not a verbatim reproduction.

## Definition

Chronic kidney disease (CKD) is defined as abnormalities of kidney structure or function present for >3 months with implications for health. Operationally, this is typically:

- eGFR <60 mL/min/1.73m² for ≥3 months, with or without markers of damage; OR
- Markers of kidney damage for ≥3 months (most commonly albuminuria), regardless of eGFR.

A single low eGFR is not chronic kidney disease.

## eGFR categories (CKD-EPI 2021, race-free)

The 2021 equation removed race as a coefficient. KDIGO categorizes eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²):

- G1 ≥90 — normal or high.
- G2 60–89 — mildly decreased.
- G3a 45–59 — moderately decreased.
- G3b 30–44 — moderately decreased.
- G4 15–29 — severely decreased.
- G5 <15 — kidney failure range.

## Albuminuria categories (urine albumin/creatinine ratio, mg/g)

- A1 <30 — normal to mildly increased.
- A2 30–300 — moderately increased.
- A3 >300 — severely increased.

Risk of progression and cardiovascular events is graded by combining eGFR and albuminuria categories (the heat-map). Higher eGFR loss with higher albuminuria = higher risk; either can drive a CKD label.

## Management priorities

- Treat underlying cause (diabetes, hypertension, primary glomerular disease, structural disease).
- Blood pressure control toward systolic <120 mmHg when tolerated, using RAS inhibitors (ACEi/ARB) when albuminuria is present.
- Glycemic control in diabetes; SGLT2 inhibitors slow progression in many CKD patients with or without diabetes.
- Lipid management per general population guidance.
- Avoid nephrotoxins where possible (NSAIDs, IV contrast caution, certain antibiotics).
- Address complications at lower eGFR: anemia, mineral-bone disorder, acidosis, fluid balance.
- Plan for kidney replacement therapy (dialysis or transplant) when eGFR approaches <15.

## Reversible mimics

A "new low eGFR" with vomiting, diuretic exposure, NSAID use, or contrast media is often acute kidney injury and partially reversible with hydration and medication review. Not every low eGFR is CKD — duration matters.

## Cystatin C

In people with very high or very low muscle mass, cystatin C-based eGFR (or the combined creatinine + cystatin C equation) is more accurate than creatinine alone.
