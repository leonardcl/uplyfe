# Lipid panel — MedlinePlus

Source: MedlinePlus, U.S. National Library of Medicine. https://medlineplus.gov/lab-tests/cholesterol-levels/

A lipid panel measures cholesterol and triglycerides in the blood and helps assess cardiovascular risk. Components typically include total cholesterol, LDL ("bad"), HDL ("good"), triglycerides, and increasingly non-HDL cholesterol.

## Adult interpretation

- Total cholesterol: under 200 mg/dL desirable, 200–239 borderline-high, 240 or above high.
- LDL cholesterol: under 100 optimal; 100–129 near-optimal; 130–159 borderline-high; 160–189 high; 190 or above very high.
- HDL cholesterol: men under 40 mg/dL is low; women under 50 mg/dL is low; 60 or above is associated with lower risk.
- Triglycerides: under 150 normal; 150–199 borderline-high; 200–499 high; 500 or above very high (risk of pancreatitis rises substantially around 1000).
- Non-HDL cholesterol (= total minus HDL): under 130 desirable; targets typically run about 30 mg/dL above the LDL goal.

## Why all four matter together

Cardiovascular risk is not driven by any single lipid in isolation. A combined picture of high LDL, high triglycerides, and low HDL — sometimes called mixed dyslipidemia — carries higher risk than any one of those values alone, particularly when paired with elevated fasting glucose, central adiposity, or elevated blood pressure (the metabolic syndrome pattern).

## What can shift the result

- Recent meals (within ~10 hours) can raise triglycerides; some labs require fasting.
- Acute illness, surgery, or recent significant weight change.
- Pregnancy raises lipids substantially.
- Some medications (corticosteroids, oral contraceptives, beta-blockers, diuretics, retinoids) alter levels.

## What to do with an abnormal result

Repeat after a clean fast and review with a clinician. Lifestyle changes — diet pattern, exercise, weight management, alcohol moderation, smoking cessation — meaningfully shift LDL, triglycerides, and HDL. Some lipid abnormalities are inherited (familial hypercholesterolemia) and require evaluation regardless of lifestyle.
