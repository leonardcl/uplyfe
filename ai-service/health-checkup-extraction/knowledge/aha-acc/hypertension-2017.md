# ACC/AHA 2017 Hypertension Guideline — summary

Source: 2017 ACC/AHA/AAPA/ABC/ACPM/AGS/APhA/ASH/ASPC/NMA/PCNA Guideline for the Prevention, Detection, Evaluation, and Management of High Blood Pressure in Adults. Hypertension. https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/HYP.0000000000000065
This is a paraphrased summary for retrieval, not a verbatim reproduction.

## Categories (mmHg)

Based on properly measured, averaged office readings (or out-of-office equivalents):

- Normal: systolic <120 AND diastolic <80.
- Elevated: systolic 120–129 AND diastolic <80.
- Stage 1 hypertension: systolic 130–139 OR diastolic 80–89.
- Stage 2 hypertension: systolic ≥140 OR diastolic ≥90.
- Hypertensive crisis: systolic ≥180 AND/OR diastolic ≥120, requiring prompt evaluation.

A single elevated reading does not make a diagnosis. The guideline emphasizes proper technique and confirmation across multiple visits or with home / ambulatory measurements.

## Why the cutoffs were lowered

The 2017 guideline reduced the diagnostic cutoff from 140/90 to 130/80 based on the SPRINT trial and a body of evidence showing reduced cardiovascular events at lower targets in higher-risk adults. The change moved more adults into a "hypertension" label and shifted more to lifestyle therapy, with pharmacologic therapy targeted to those with established cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, or 10-year ASCVD risk ≥10%.

## Lifestyle therapy

Recommended for all adults with elevated or stage 1 hypertension, and as adjunct to medication for stage 2:

- Weight reduction in those with overweight or obesity.
- Heart-healthy diet pattern (DASH).
- Sodium reduction toward 1500–2300 mg/day.
- Increased dietary potassium (3500–5000 mg/day from food) unless contraindicated.
- Regular aerobic activity (90–150 minutes/week) plus dynamic resistance training.
- Alcohol moderation (≤2 drinks/day men, ≤1/day women).

## Hypertensive crisis

Systolic ≥180 or diastolic ≥120, particularly with chest pain, shortness of breath, vision changes, neurologic deficits, or severe headache, is a medical emergency. Evaluation should not be delayed.

## Measurement quality

The guideline gives weight to measurement technique: rest 5 minutes, feet flat, back supported, arm at heart level, no caffeine or smoking 30 min before, average two readings on two visits. Many "high" readings in a clinic vanish with proper technique or out-of-office monitoring.
