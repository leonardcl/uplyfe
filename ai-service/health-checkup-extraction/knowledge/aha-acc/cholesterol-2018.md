# AHA/ACC 2018 Cholesterol Guideline — summary

Source: 2018 AHA/ACC/Multisociety Guideline on the Management of Blood Cholesterol. Circulation. https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000625
This is a paraphrased summary for retrieval, not a verbatim reproduction.

## Headline framing

The guideline reinforces that LDL is the primary target of cholesterol management, that risk should be assessed with the pooled cohort 10-year ASCVD risk estimator in adults 40–75, and that lifestyle therapy is the foundation for everyone, with pharmacologic therapy layered on by risk category.

## Risk groups (paraphrased)

- Severe primary hypercholesterolemia: LDL ≥190 mg/dL — high risk regardless of calculated 10-year risk.
- Established clinical atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD): high risk.
- Diabetes mellitus, age 40–75: moderate-to-high risk depending on additional factors.
- Adults 40–75 without ASCVD or diabetes, LDL 70–189 mg/dL: estimate 10-year ASCVD risk and use it for shared decision making about therapy.

## LDL targets and "percent reduction" framing

The guideline frames management in terms of percent LDL reduction rather than fixed treat-to-target numbers:

- High-intensity therapy: aim for ≥50% LDL reduction.
- Moderate-intensity therapy: aim for 30–49% LDL reduction.

Practical screening cutoffs for *interpretation* (not treatment by themselves):

- LDL <100 mg/dL — optimal for the general adult population.
- LDL 100–129 — near-optimal.
- LDL 130–159 — borderline-high.
- LDL 160–189 — high.
- LDL ≥190 — very high (severe primary hypercholesterolemia).

## Risk-enhancing factors

When 10-year risk is borderline or intermediate, the guideline lists "risk enhancers" that nudge toward more intensive therapy: family history of premature ASCVD, persistently elevated LDL ≥160, chronic kidney disease, metabolic syndrome, conditions specific to women (preeclampsia, premature menopause), chronic inflammatory conditions, ethnicity (e.g., South Asian), persistently elevated triglycerides, and elevated lipid biomarkers including hs-CRP, Lp(a), apolipoprotein B, and ankle-brachial index.

## Lifestyle therapy

The guideline anchors on a heart-healthy diet pattern, regular physical activity, weight management, smoking cessation, and reduction of saturated fat. AHA's accompanying lifestyle guidance recommends limiting saturated fat to less than ~6% of total calories.

## Patient-clinician discussion

Treatment decisions are made jointly: the guideline emphasizes shared decision making — discussing 10-year and lifetime risk, the magnitude of benefit, potential adverse effects, and patient preferences before committing to long-term therapy.
