# NCEP ATP III — lipid screening cutoffs

Source: National Cholesterol Education Program, Adult Treatment Panel III. NIH Publication.
This is a paraphrased summary for retrieval, not a verbatim reproduction.

ATP III is the long-standing reference for lipid panel interpretation cutoffs. The AHA/ACC 2018 guideline updated treatment framing (percent reduction by risk group), but ATP III's cutoffs remain widely used for *describing* lipid panels in routine reports.

## Total cholesterol (mg/dL)

- Below 200 — desirable.
- 200–239 — borderline-high.
- 240 or above — high.

## LDL cholesterol (mg/dL)

- Below 100 — optimal.
- 100–129 — near-optimal / above optimal.
- 130–159 — borderline-high.
- 160–189 — high.
- 190 or above — very high.

## HDL cholesterol (mg/dL)

- Below 40 (men) or below 50 (women) — low; associated with higher cardiovascular risk.
- 60 or above — protective; reduces risk.

## Triglycerides (mg/dL)

- Below 150 — normal.
- 150–199 — borderline-high.
- 200–499 — high.
- 500 or above — very high. Risk of acute pancreatitis rises substantially around 1000 mg/dL.

## Non-HDL cholesterol

Non-HDL is total cholesterol minus HDL. It captures all atherogenic particles (LDL, VLDL remnants, IDL, Lp(a)). Targets typically run about 30 mg/dL above the LDL goal:

- Below 130 — desirable.
- 130–159 — above desirable.
- 160–189 — borderline-high.
- 190–219 — high.
- 220 or above — very high.

## Metabolic syndrome (ATP III definition)

Three or more of the following define the metabolic syndrome pattern:

- Waist circumference: men ≥102 cm (40 in), women ≥88 cm (35 in).
- Triglycerides ≥150 mg/dL (or on triglyceride-lowering therapy).
- HDL <40 mg/dL men, <50 mg/dL women.
- Blood pressure ≥130/85 mmHg (or on antihypertensive therapy).
- Fasting glucose ≥100 mg/dL (or on glucose-lowering therapy).

The pattern carries higher cardiovascular and diabetes risk than any one component in isolation.
